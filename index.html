What is E.V.E. Systems?

E.V.E. (Emergent Virtual Environment) is the interaction surface for your Umbra-Noesis stack. It defines how windows, panels, timelines, and tools are arranged to prioritize:

  • Fast, natural interaction with local AI (text, voice, and future modalities).
  • Context-aware layouts that follow how you work, not how software ships.
  • Stable, repeatable environments across different PCe profiles and nodes.

Role inside the ecosystem

E.V.E. is where the Umbra-Noesis stack becomes visible and tangible: dashboards, consoles, sidebars, and scenes that sit on top of EVAA, AACP, and UmbraOS. It’s the layer that lets a captain, developer, or analyst walk up and immediately understand what their system is doing.

Founders / Lab

Experimental layouts for deep research, protocol views, and stack introspection.

Field / Mobile

Streamlined, distraction-aware UIs for MobileCore-style rigs and on-watch usage.

Client / PCe Kits

Future turnkey interface profiles that ship with PCe reference builds or licenses.

E.V.E. Roadmap (High Level)

  • Core Layout Prototypes — internal E.V.E. shells for lab and MobileCore use, used as the baseline interaction patterns.
  • Profiles & Scenes — documented UI profiles for captains, builders, analysts, and everyday users.
  • E.V.E. Product Line — future UI packs, themes, and licensed interface designs aligned with Umbra-Noesis hardware and OS.

Integration Points

E.V.E. is designed to sit on top of:

  • UmbraOS as the host environment for windows, services, and sessions.
  • EVAA Cognition as the resident local AI and reasoning engine.
  • AACP as the underlying interaction and protocol layer for requests and responses.
  • PCe Systems as the physical nodes where these experiences actually live.

This page is an early structural skeleton. Final UI previews, interaction demos, and licensing details will be added as E.V.E. moves from internal prototypes to public-ready profiles.
